## **Stark — annotation guidance**

Adjective: **stark**  
Category: **Problem**

---

### **1. Value-judgement**  
Yes – evaluates severity / salience.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Despite these **stark** discrepancies and poor overall outcomes in YA, very little research has focused on these high-risk YA with T1D.*

</details>

---

### **2. Hyperbolic**  
No.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**  
Usually gratuitous in attributive constructions – typical nouns: *contrast, difference, disparity, fact, lack, pattern, reality, statistic, evidence, relief, shift, deficit, discrepancy, efficacy, example, exception, reminder, abnormality, absence, boundary, contradiction, departure, diminution, division, gap*.

As predicate, possible non-gratuitous.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *In **stark** contrast, broad NAbs (b NAbs) from HIV-1+ donors frequently contact glycans rather than avoid them.* (gratuitous)

- *The contrast to countries without liver flukes is **stark** given that incidence of CCA is less than 3 per 100,000 elsewhere (USA, 1.67 ASR in 2014).* (non-gratuitous)

</details>

---
