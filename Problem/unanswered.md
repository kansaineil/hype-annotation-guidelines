## **Unanswered — annotation guidance**

Adjective: **unanswered**  
Category: **Problem**

---

### **1. Value-judgement**  
Yes, usually weakly evaluative: adds rhetorical emphasis, framing a gap as salient / important.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Findings from this effort will inform **unanswered** questions concerning long-term, cumulative, developmental effects of pediatric BP on adult psychiatric, functional, and health status, as well as the development of novel preventive strategies to enhance long-term management.*

</details>

---

### **2. Hyperbolic**  
No.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**  
Usually gratuitous in attributive constructions. Head nouns typically encode the meaning of “unanswered” (e.g. *question, issue, challenge, area, hypothesis, gap, mystery, problem, puzzle, concern, need*).  
As predicate, may be non-gratuitous or gratuitous – ask whether it can be deleted.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *NORDIC will provide the organization and operational capability to perform multi-site observational and treatment trials, involving nearly 200 community and academic practitioners, in order to address the many crucial yet **unanswered** questions about risks, diagnosis, treatment and other management aspects of these diseases that could not be studied without a clinical research organization.* (gratuitous)

- *Several fundamental questions remain **unanswered**.* (gratuitous)

- *Although the artemisinin-combination therapies (ACTs) are the most important drugs for the treatment of uncomplicated malaria, fundamental questions are **unanswered**, including which ACT choice and dose is best for HIV-infected and HIV-uninfected children.* (non-gratuitous)

</details>

Unlikely.

### **3c. Broader context**  
Possible.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *These are crucial and **unanswered** questions in EoE.*

</details>

---

### **3d. Grounds**  
Possible – usually signaled by reference to prior context (e.g. *thus*).

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Thus, many questions remain **unanswered** with regard to the impact of selective CD28 blockade during transplantation.*

</details>

---

### **3e. Relative / Hedged**  
Hedging possible with *largely*.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Three research questions that remain largely **unanswered** in the childhood asthma literature and in our Children’s Health Study (CHS) motivate this proposal: First, do social, economic, and demographic variables and indicators of stress confound or modify the relationship between air pollution and the incidence of wheeze or asthma?*

</details>
