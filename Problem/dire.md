### **1. Value-judgement**  
Almost always yes.

<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *When these considerations are understood against the backdrop of childhood hearing disorders, the **dire** implications for Palestine are magnified.*

### **2. Hyperbolic**  
Usually yes.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *There is a **dire** need for repeatable and clinically relevant outcome measures to be developed so future studies can investigate new therapeutics.*

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**  
Often gratuitous in attributive phrases where the core proposition stands without it (*dire need, dire consequences, dire situation*).

Typical head nouns: *need, consequence, consequences, prognosis, outcome, outcomes, situation, shortage, implication, implications, concern, complications, effect, effects, statistic, statistics, circumstance, circumstances, threat, threats, risk, toxicity, problem, state*.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Nursing administrators are in **dire** need of research results to inform their practice.*

- *Therefore, understanding the etiological basis for the risk of suicide is of **dire** importance to our Veteran population.*

</details>
