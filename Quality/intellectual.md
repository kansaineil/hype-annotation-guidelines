### **1. Value-judgement**  

Mixed.

Usually YES when *intellectual* is used to put a person, environment, or community in a good light.  

Typical targets: *environment, resources, merit, exchange, leadership, expertise, community, interactions, infrastructure, home, stimulation, growth, climate, vitality, promise, talents.*

Usually NO when *intellectual* is used in established clinical/technical expressions.  

Typical targets: *disability, disabilities, functioning, ability, abilities, function, impairment, impairments, deficits, decline, deterioration, deficiency, disorder, disorders, retardation.*

<details markdown="1">

<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The forum is truly international, with participants from around the world, which creates a superb **intellectual** environment.* (YES)

- *Data were gathered on an extensive battery of tests of **intellectual** functioning.* (NO)

</details>

### **3a. Gratuitous**  

Often YES in the promotional sense, especially with general positive institutional nouns.  

Typical targets: *environment, resources, exchange, community, climate, stimulation, vitality, promise.*

<details markdown="1">

<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *In addition to bringing clinical research training under a single umbrella, the Institute will create a vibrant **intellectual** community of accomplished senior mentors, junior faculty, and research scholars who will undertake crossdisciplinary, cutting-edge translational research.*

</details>
