### **1. Value-judgement**  

Often YES.

YES — when duration is used to make the referent seem more important, consequential, credible, or worthy of attention.  

Typical targets: *collaboration, commitment, research, program, expertise, tradition, track record, gap, problem, question, issue, debate, importance.*

NO — when it mainly indicates duration in a more neutral descriptive way, especially with diseases or conditions.  

Typical targets: *disease, hypertension, diabetes, symptoms, deficits, impairment.*

<details markdown="1">

<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *This proposal continues our **longstanding** interest in the role of metals in the antitumor properties of compounds which can bind metal ions.* (YES)

- *Recent histopathologic studies suggest that extensive cortical gray matter damage is present in patients with **longstanding** disease.* (NO)

</details>
