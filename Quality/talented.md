### **1. Value-judgement**  

Almost always YES — positively evaluates ability or potential.

Typical targets: *faculty, investigators, researchers, scientists, students, trainees, mentors, team, group, cohort.*

Examples:  

*talented investigators*  

*a talented and diverse group of individuals*

<details markdown="1">

<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Strengths on which we can capitalize include significant funded renovation and expansion projects, a research focus (infectious diseases and gene therapy) that matches NIH and national priorities, faculty appointments, strong and diverse research resources, a **talented** and dedicated staff, excellent interactions and collaborations with area universities and an outstanding relationship with our host institution and state and federal legislators.*

</details>

### **3a. Gratuitous**  

Usually YES — often removable without changing the core meaning.

*talented investigators* → *investigators*  

*a talented group of mentors* → *a group of mentors*

<details markdown="1">

<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *With these funds, a **talented** faculty integrated through cancer center mechanisms will make advances in the prevention, early detection, and treatment of cancer in North Carolina and the nation.*

</details>

---

### **3b. Amplified**  

Possible — e.g. *highly talented*, *exceptionally talented*, *most talented*.

<details markdown="1">

<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Finally, we have appointed extremely **talented** trainees and maintained an equal balance between clinical and PhD trainees over the past funding cycle.*

</details>

---

### **3c. Broader context**  

Often YES — frequently appears in stacked positive descriptions.

Examples: *talented and diverse*, *talented and experienced*, *dedicated and talented.*

<details markdown="1">

<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The Program will recruit and retain **talented**, diverse students through innovative and dedicated coordination with the University and partner schools and be evaluated formally by a Board of Directors and an external assessor.*

</details>
