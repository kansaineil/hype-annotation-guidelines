---

### **1. Value-judgement**  
Almost always YES — used to present an environment or group in a positive light (cooperative, supportive).

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Although other meetings exist in the US that present various aspects of bacterial pathogenesis or prokaryotic biology, this is the only meeting focusing specifically on Gram-positive pathogenesis and biology, and which provides a **collegial** and supporting environment that fosters interactions between established investigators and junior scientists.*

</details>

---

### **2. Hyperbolic**  
No.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**  
Usually yes.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The **collegial** atmosphere and the ample free time in the afternoons and late evenings provide ideal settings for participants from different disciplines to brainstorm and establish interdisciplinary collaborations.* (yes)

- *Over the past several years, investigators in the Department of Ophthalmology have shared resources, technical expertise, and equipment to facilitate their respective research programs in a remarkably **collegial** atmosphere.* (yes – deletion of shell noun frame)

</details>

---

### **3b. Amplified**  
Occasionally.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The 2-yearly NOX Gordon Conference is firmly established as the premier forum for the presentation and discussion of the latest advances in the field in a highly **collegial**, collaborative, and informal atmosphere.*

</details>

---

### **3c. Broader context**  
Probable.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *An excellent **collegial** environment and adequate support services exist within the local University community.*

</details>

---

### **3d. Grounds**  
Probable – often used in adjective stack.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *At the graduate level the TPCN offers a PhD program in Neural Computation (PNC) and joint PhD programs with CMU’s Department of Statistics (PNC-Stat) and its Machine Learning Department (PNC-MLD), all set within a highly **collegial**, cross-disciplinary environment of our Center for the Neural Basis of Cognition (CNBC), which is operated jointly by CMU and Pitt.*

</details>

---

Usually no.
