### **1. Value-judgement**  

Mixed.

YES — when used to positively evaluate people in terms of competence or suitability.  

Typical targets: *investigators, researchers, personnel, team, scientists, mentors, staff.*

NO — when used mainly to indicate formal eligibility or selection criteria.  

Typical targets: *participants, applicants, students, trainees, patients, subjects, users.*

Examples:  

*qualified investigators* (value-judgement)  

*qualified participants for the study* (non-value judgement)

<details markdown="1">

<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The SASC is co-directed by Dr. David Colcher, a Professor in the Department of Molecular Immunology, and Dr. Jun Wu, an Associate Research Professor of Comparative Medicine, and is supported by highly **qualified** staff that maintain and operate the instrumentation.* (YES)

- *To address this need, we propose to develop a tele-exercise system that allows a **qualified** therapist to remotely supervise and interact with the patient during goal-oriented game-like and low risk exercise tasks.* (YES – gratuitous)

- *The purpose of the Neuropathology Core is to establish diagnoses on all brains submitted to the Tissue Resource Center, store fixed and frozen brain tissue and distribute brain tissue to **qualified** investigators.* (NO)

- *Qualified patients will be randomly selected for the study and randomly assigned to either clozapine or usual care.* (NO)

</details>
