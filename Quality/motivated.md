### **1. Value-judgement**  

Mixed.

NO (dominant use) — when motivated is used in a technical/psychological sense meaning “driven by underlying processes”.  

Typical targets: *behavior(s), attention, cognition, responding, decision, reasoning, performance, processing.*

→ Here it is descriptive, not evaluative.

YES — when used to positively evaluate people or groups as engaged, committed, or desirable.  

Typical targets: *students, trainees, scientists, investigators, candidates, faculty, team, volunteers, participants.*

<details markdown="1">

<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Application of a memory retrieval schema to an analysis of extinction of aversively **motivated** behavior.* (NO)

- *Talented and **motivated** students may continue their program experience during the ensuing year.* (YES)

</details>
