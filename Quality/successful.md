### **1. Value-judgement**  

Mixed, but often YES.

YES — Note that when *successful* can be removed and the core meaning remains essentially unchanged, it often adds positive gloss rather than necessary content.

Examples:  

- *obstacles to successful collaboration* → *obstacles to collaboration*  

- *successful completion of the aims* → *completion of the aims*

NO — when *successful* is needed to report whether an outcome was achieved.

Example:  

- *the procedure was successful in 46 patients* → removal changes meaning.

<details markdown="1">

<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *If **successful**, this study will produce a diagnostic test that is efficient, effective, affordable, culturally acceptable, and deliverable to sites that serve health disparity populations.* (NO)

- *In the Case Western Reserve University/Cleveland Clinic CTSA (the CTSA) we will coordinate existing resources relevant to clinical research at Case Western Reserve University and three of its hospital affiliates, the Cleveland Clinic, MetroHealth Medical Center, and University Hospitals Case Medical Center, including three existing GGRC facilities, a **successful** multidisciplinary institutional K12 program, substantial technological and statistical core facilities that currently exist in silos, and our famous practice-based research networks.* (YES)

</details>
