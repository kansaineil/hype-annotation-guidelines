### **1. Value-judgement**  

YES — when used to positively evaluate expertise or capability.  

Typical targets: *researcher; clinician; team; staff; investigator.*

NO — when used descriptively to indicate prior exposure or background without clear praise.  

Typical targets: *participants; users; patients; group (e.g. experienced vs novice in experimental setup).*

<details markdown="1">

<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The strength of our core lies in our **experienced** core investigators with complementary expertise/experience and exceptional devotion to mentoring and education of junior investigators.* (YES)

- *Twenty **experienced** marijuana smokers living on a research ward for 25 days will be given repeated doses of oral THC at doses to produce two levels of tolerance (10 mg and 20 mg doses).* (NO)

</details>

### **3a. Gratuitous**  

Often YES.

<details markdown="1">

<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *An **experienced** team of oncologic physicians and nurses will collaborate closely with a full range of medical and surgical specialists.*

</details>

### **3b. Amplified**  

Often YES.

<details markdown="1">

<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The teaching staff (mentors) is composed of members of the University of Pennsylvania staff who are very **experienced** with the proven ability to publish and obtain independent funding for their work.*

</details>

### **3c. Broader context**  

Possible.

<details markdown="1">

<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Four groups of **experienced**, productive, interactive researchers will investigate a wide range of issues for further advancement of this approach.*

</details>
