### **1. Value-judgement**
Yes. Typically modifies:

- Abstract outcomes / states: *impact, potential, success, importance, benefit, value*
- Problems / negatives: *gap, challenge, burden, cost, risk, need, toll*
- Quantities (usually impressionistic): *number, amount, quantities, volume*
- Collectives / domains: *population, market, research, data, background*
- Processes / change: *increase, advance, expansion, growth*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The success of this project will have a **huge** impact on medicine and could change treatment strategies in many medical and surgical ischemic conditions.*

</details>

---

### **2. Hyperbolic**
Yes when **huge** modifies an abstract head noun directly (*huge impact/gap/potential/need*).

Also yes (but less so) when it modifies quantifier/container nouns (*huge number/amount/range/variety*).

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Vestibular disorders exact a **huge** toll in lost productivity and quality of life.*

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**
Usually yes

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Because solvents encompass a **huge** range of disparate compounds, association with any particular agent is likely to be obscured using this approach.*

</details>
