### **1. Value-judgement**
Usually yes - evaluates scope as a virtue (≈ thorough, wide-ranging, complete)

Common exceptions are proper nouns (e.g. “the Yale Comprehensive Cancer Center”).

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *This project will provide the first **comprehensive** understanding of diet effects on apoprotein synthesis in nonhuman primates and may reveal correlations between parameters of diet responsiveness and / or atherosclerosis susceptibility and apoprotein synthetic rates.*

</details>

### **3a. Gratuitous**
Usually yes – almost always modifies nouns that imply scope or coverage. Common nouns:

- Epistemic / cognitive: *understanding, knowledge, picture, view, information, description*
- Analytical / research processes: *analysis, assessment, evaluation, investigation, study, studies, research, characterization, review*
- Structures / abstractions: *approach, model, framework, strategy, program, system, plan*
- Resources: *data, database, set, array, list, series, battery*
- Institutional / service labels: *training, care, treatment, intervention, services, center, curriculum*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *A broad range of education, training, and career development programs will continue to meet research workforce needs.*

- *We have begun **comprehensive** analyses of two systems.*

</details>
