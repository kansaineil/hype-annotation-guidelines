### **1. Value-judgement**
Yes. **Tremendous** evaluates magnitude as a virtue/problem signal, typically with abstract/collective nouns (*potential, impact, progress, promise, need, burden, value, success, benefit, implications, growth, interest, importance*).

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *This highly innovative new screening test has **tremendous** potential for commercial and clinical applications which include: AIDS screening -- to complement and pre-date HIV positivity, blood donor screening -- to exclude acute infectious disease or catastrophic chronic illness when to symptoms are apparent; and transplant rejection -- to detect rejection episodes prior to clinical symptoms.*

</details>

---

### **2. Hyperbolic**
Yes

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Given the **tremendous** impact of cardiovascular disease on public health, the possibility that air pollution plays a significant role in the exacerbation of these conditions has major ramifications.*

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**
If can be deleted without change in the meaning

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Given the **tremendous** growth in mass spectrometry-based applications for biomolecule analysis, new technology that could further improve on small molecule analysis and compatibility with microfluidics and silicon microchip technology as well as impurity tolerance, sensitivity, and automation will have an enormous impact in the biological sciences.* (gratuitous)

- *There is a **tremendous** amount of collaboration between individuals in Neurology, Neurosurgery, Radiology, Psychiatry, and Endocrinology in the development of a variety of innovative approaches to imaging the central nervous system in patients with a variety of neuropsychological disorders, an important theme of the Center.* (not gratuitous â€“ but still value judgement)

</details>
