### **1. Value-judgement**
YES – almost always value judgement (usually gratuitous).

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *There will be **ample** time for the presentations and discussion.*

- *The results generated should provide **ample**, powerful experimental systems for future studies on the biological processes of these organisms in their ecological niche.*

</details>

### **3a. Gratuitous**
Usually gratuitous – typical nouns modified by **ample**:

- Epistemic / evidential nouns: *ample evidence, ample data, ample information*
- Opportunity / feasibility nouns: *ample opportunity, ample access, ample reason*
- Resource / support nouns: *ample resources, ample funding, ample support*
- Abstract capacity nouns: *ample experience, ample expertise, ample capacity*
- Quantitative nouns without measurement: *ample time, ample quantities, ample numbers*
- Physical / spatial nouns (borderline): *ample space, ample room*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *There is **ample** evidence that calcium plays a central role in the activation of lymphocytes and the production of lymphokines.*

</details>
