### **1. Value-judgement**
Yes. Often modifies abstract, impressionistic nouns, e.g. *potential, impact, value, importance, burden, benefit, promise, gap, need, relevance, significance, opportunity*.

Sometimes modifies quantitative container nouns â€“ e.g. *variety, range*.

<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Latency in HIV infection has **immense** importance because infectious virus can remain protected from immune mediated clearance and re-emerge after a long absence, often years following initial infection, regardless of current therapeutic strategies.*

### **2. Hyperbolic**
Yes when **immense** modifies an abstract head noun directly (*immense impact / gap / potential / importance*).

Also yes when it modifies nouns denoting measurable quantities (*immense variety, range*).

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Despite their **immense** impact on human health, relatively little is known about the genetics of Chlamydia pathogenesis, because no genetic techniques exist to create targeted mutants in these organisms.*

</details>
