### **1. Value-judgement**
Value judgement when **diverse** modifies abstract, organizational, or epistemic nouns. Diversity treated as intrinsic virtue, typically no explicit dimension is given. Typical noun types:

- Institutional / organizational: *team, workforce, faculty, community, cadre, panel*
- Epistemic / academic: *research, disciplines, fields, expertise, perspectives, approaches, projects*
- Programmatic / social: *training, resources, initiatives, stakeholders, audience*

Not a value judgement when modifies countable biological, demographic, or material entities. Typical noun types:

- Populations / samples: *population(s), cohort(s), sample(s), participants*
- Biological entities: *species, organisms, tissues, strains, pathogens, cells*
- Mechanistic/material sets: *compounds, ligands, pathways, phenotypes, stimuli*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *To ensure that the intervention is culturally appropriate to farmers, they have brought together a **diverse** team of investigators and consultants with expertise in tobacco policy, community organization, agricultural economics, and tobacco farming.* (Value judgement)

- *Texas' large and ethnically **diverse** population will enable the calculation of incidence and prevalence measures for a geographically defined area and for black, hispanic and white populations.* (Non-value judgement)

</details>
