## **Tangible — annotation guidance**

Adjective: **tangible**  
Category: **UTILITY**

---

### **1. Value-judgement**

Usually YES — *tangible* positively frames something as capable of producing real-world impact. Common targets include *benefits, outcomes, results, support, evidence, strategies, therapies, advances, products, improvements,* and *resources.*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Cancer clinical trials are essential to translating research into **tangible** benefits for patients.* (YES)

- *The project will improve our understanding of age-related muscle changes in African Americans, yield **tangible** outcomes such as the development of a non-invasive portable sarcopenia and myosteatosis measure.* (YES)

</details>

---

### **2. Hyperbolic**

No.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**

Mixed.

YES when *tangible* is removable and mainly adds positive framing:

- *tangible benefits → benefits*  
- *tangible outcomes → outcomes*  
- *tangible support → support*  
- *tangible strategies → strategies*  

NO when concreteness itself is the key contrast or criterion (e.g. abstract vs. concrete, physical vs. intangible, measurable vs. conceptual).

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The proposed research will yield **tangible** outcomes in the form of new neurostimulation design methodologies and a benchmark control model.* (YES)

- *The pilot projects offer a **tangible** mechanism for promoting research in priority areas.* (YES)

- *Mentorship offers **tangible** benefits … and less tangible benefits, such as endorsed opportunities to network with senior people in the field.* (NO)

- *The costs incurred by patients and caregivers, both **tangible** and intangible, are a major threat to public health.* (NO)

- *The typically quantitative and abstract models are connected to the more **tangible** notions the biologist has about gene regulation mechanisms.* (NO)

</details>

### **3c. Broader context**

Common.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Translational oncology embodies the central philosophy of UCSD Cancer Center, which places the highest priority and emphasis on converting the discoveries from high quality research into **tangible** improvements in prevention, diagnosis, treatment and amelioration of discomfort for the benefit of the community.*

</details>

---

### **3d. Grounds**

Common — often accompanied by explicit justification.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *These encouraging statistics provide **tangible** evidence of the broad interest and commitment of the scientific community.* (YES)

- ***Tangible** outcomes include a precise near-bp map of genomic locations and sub-complex organization.* (YES)

</details>

---

### **3e. Relative / Hedged**

Possible but uncommon.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *This study has the potential of establishing a **tangible** way of assessing severity of ICH.*

</details>
