## **Scalable — annotation guidance**

Adjective: **scalable**  
Category: **UTILITY**

---

### **1. Value-judgement**

Usually YES when *scalable* means suitable for wider use.  

NO when it is purely technical or descriptive, especially formal terms such as *Scalable Vector Graphics.*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *This study will evaluate a highly-**scalable** strategy for increasing social engagement and preventing cognitive decline among older adults with MCI.* (YES)

</details>

---

### **2. Hyperbolic**

No.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**

YES when *scalable* can be removed and the main claim remains — often with *strategy, intervention, platform, pipeline,* or *model.*  

NO when scalability is the main research problem, comparison, or technical capability being tested.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The overarching goal of Vivonoetics, Inc. is to develop a **scalable** informatics platform to conduct advanced physiological monitoring and processing of data from wearable sensors.* → *develop an informatics platform…* (YES)

- *Manual data extraction is laborious, resource intensive, and, therefore, not **scalable**.* (NO)

</details>

---

### **3b. Amplified**

YES — commonly boosted by *highly, readily, broadly,* and *fully.*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *This project will test a highly **scalable** model of sleep care in a large primary care system to determine the potential for our stepped care model on MDD prevention.*

</details>

---

### **3c. Broader context**

YES when stacked with other positive utility terms such as *sustainable, accessible, cost-effective, robust, practical, effective, sharable,* or *generalizable.*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The need for cost-effective and **scalable** screening and treatment strategies to reduce ICC is therefore substantial.*

</details>

---

### **3d. Grounds**

YES when the sentence explains why something is scalable.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *This approach is highly **scalable** because reagent and instrumentation costs are modest (now a few tens of thousands of dollars).*

</details>

---

### **3e. Relative / Hedged**

YES when limited by *potentially, may be, if successful, readily, perhaps,* or by comparison with another approach.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Digital mental health interventions (DMHIs), delivered via the internet and/or mobile apps, have evolved as efficacious and potentially **scalable** treatments.*

</details>
