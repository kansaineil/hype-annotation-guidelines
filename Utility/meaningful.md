## **Meaningful — annotation guidance**

Adjective: **meaningful**  
Category: **UTILITY**

---

### **1. Value-judgement**

Almost always YES. *Meaningful* evaluates usefulness.  
Possible NO when it simply means *having semantic content.*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The objective of this research project is to develop a reliable, valid and clinically **meaningful** computer-derived measure of asbestos-induced pleural and parenchymal lung disease using HRCT.* (YES)

- *Performance using material containing familiar, **meaningful** words was compared with that using nonsense syllable production.* (NO)

</details>

---

### **2. Hyperbolic**

No.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**

YES when removable without changing the core claim.  
NO when *meaningful* forms part of an established criterion or threshold (especially *clinically meaningful*).

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Central to the achievement of this goal is a faculty capable of quality, **meaningful** research in a well-supported research infrastructure.* (YES)

- *The trial will have 80 percent power to detect clinically **meaningful** differences between the groups on all primary outcomes.* (NO)

</details>

---

### **3b. Amplified**

Possible.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3c. Broader context**

YES — common.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *This technology is essential in deconvoluting fluorophores with overlapping excitation or emission spectra so that **meaningful** quantitative information from each dye can be determined at subcellular resolution.*

</details>

---

### **3d. Grounds**

Possible.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The estimation that 70 subjects will need to complete the protocol to obtain **meaningful** results is based on several assumptions, including the precision of the procedure.*

</details>

---

### **3e. Relative / Hedged**

Often YES. May be bounded — e.g. *clinically meaningful, statistically meaningful, biologically meaningful, functionally meaningful,* or *personally meaningful.*

May be negated — e.g. *too small to allow*, *too limited for*.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The number of deaths in the female component remains too small to allow **meaningful** analysis at this time.*

</details>
