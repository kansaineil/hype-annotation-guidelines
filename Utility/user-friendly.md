## **User-friendly — annotation guidance**

Adjective: **user-friendly**  
Category: **UTILITY**

---

### **1. Value-judgement**

Often YES — *user-friendly* positively evaluates usability.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *We will develop a **user-friendly** software package called PrecisionX that will be freely available.* (YES)

- *eSNAP is an intuitive, **user-friendly** social network visualization tool designed for clinical use.* (YES)

- *The proposal aims to develop point-of-care **user-friendly** diagnostics that can be used by patients at home.* (YES)

</details>

---

### **2. Hyperbolic**

NO.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**

Mixed — often removable without affecting factual content:

- *user-friendly software → software*  
- *user-friendly tutorials → tutorials*  

However, usability itself is sometimes a central contribution.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *We will provide **user-friendly** online tutorials.* → *tutorials* (YES)

- *Open-access **user-friendly** software will be developed and distributed.* (YES)

- *Current pipelines are not **user-friendly** or customizable, which is a serious obstacle prohibiting clinician use.* (NO)

- *The most important roadblock is the lack of **user-friendly** and scalable software tools.* (NO)

</details>

<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Simple, **user-friendly**, evidence-based tool...*

</details>
