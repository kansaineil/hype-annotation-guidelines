## **Efficient — annotation guidance**

Adjective: **efficient**  
Category: **UTILITY**

---

### **1. Value-judgement**

Usually YES — positively evaluates economy of time, cost, effort, or resources.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Further refinement will provide a rapid, **efficient** method for screening large numbers of samples from human tissues.*

</details>

---

### **2. Hyperbolic**

No.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**

Mixed. Use the deletion test strictly.

Mark YES when *efficient* can be removed without changing the core claim, especially in broad aspirational phrases.

Mark NO when efficiency is the central claim, a design requirement, a comparison, or a measured/technical property.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

**YES**

- *Such knowledge will contribute to development of **efficient** procedures for the construction of architecturally novel antitumor agents.*

- *This system also allows **efficient** 3-D dynamic imaging of physiological processes.*

**NO**

- *How **efficient** is the support treatment?*

- *Cluster analysis alone is **efficient** when it is applied to only a few thousand events in a list mode file.*

</details>

---

### **3b. Amplified**

Sometimes YES — especially with *highly, very,* or *maximally*.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *A highly **efficient** procedure for transferring functional genes into mammalian cells has been developed using retroviral vectors.*

- *New, very **efficient** methods have been developed in these laboratories to simultaneously solve both problems.*

</details>

---

### **3c. Broader context**

Often YES — frequently stacked with other utility terms such as *rapid, accurate, cost-effective, effective, selective, simple, robust, timely,* and *productive.*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Further refinement will provide a rapid, **efficient** method for screening large numbers of samples from human tissues.*

- *The results are expected to offer major insights into the development of more effective and **efficient** strategies for improving oral health.*

</details>

---

### **3d. Grounds**

Often YES — commonly grounded through design features, prior development, comparison, demonstrated performance, or stated mechanism.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Concentration of activities necessary for a large number of projects in a Core Area enables **efficient** use of personnel, equipment and space and enhances interactions among investigators.*

- *A highly **efficient** procedure for transferring functional genes into mammalian cells has been developed using retroviral vectors.*

</details>

---

### **3e. Relative / Hedged**

Common — often appears in comparisons, questions, conditionals, or bounded technical claims.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *develop methods for more **efficient** analysis of data*

- *the most **efficient** method for doing so*

- *How **efficient** is the support treatment?*

- *if DFMO proves to be an **efficient** drug to treat murine lupus...*

</details>
