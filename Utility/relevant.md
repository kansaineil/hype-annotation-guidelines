## **Relevant — annotation guidance**

Adjective: **relevant**  
Category: **UTILITY**

---

### **1. Value-judgement**

YES when *relevant* means useful.  

NO when it only marks a technical relation.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The proposed studies are likely to provide biologically **relevant** and medically significant information on host defenses in malignancies and virus infections.* (YES)

- *The **relevant** cells are granulocytes.* (NO)

</details>

---

### **2. Hyperbolic**

No.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**

Use the deletion test. Look at the noun that *relevant* modifies — does deletion leave the meaning relatively unchanged?

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Data entry and data management personnel will ensure accurate recording of all **relevant** information and speedy transmission of this data to investigators for IRB-approved studies.*

</details>

---

### **3b. Amplified**

Often YES — amplification occurs when a modifier increases the strength or certainty of relevance.

Common examples include *highly relevant, very relevant, extremely relevant, particularly relevant,* and *especially relevant.*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Because of close similarities between the rhesus monkey's and the human visual system, the data are highly **relevant** for the clinical management of human visual system disorders like amblyopia, strabismus and congenital cataracts.*

</details>
