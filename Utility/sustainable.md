## **Sustainable — annotation guidance**

Adjective: **sustainable**  
Category: **UTILITY**

---

### **1. Value-judgement**

Usually YES. *Sustainable* generally carries a positive utility evaluation because it implies durability.  

NO when descriptive in environmental or policy contexts (e.g. *Sustainable Development Goals*).

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The overarching goal of the NECN is to develop effective and **sustainable** interventions for substance use disorders that can be delivered in multiple healthcare settings.* (YES)

- *Given the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) in which United Nations has resolved to reduce morbidity…* (NO)

</details>

---

### **2. Hyperbolic**

No.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**

YES when *sustainable* can be removed and the main claim remains unchanged (e.g. *intervention, program, network, infrastructure, capacity,* or *partnership*).  

NO when long-term continuation is itself the central problem or contribution.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The goal of this two-year grant is to develop a viable and **sustainable** plan for the GEOHealth Hub…* → *develop a plan…* (YES)

- *This redeployment of staff is not **sustainable**, and the resulting costs and lost revenue are imposing an unacceptable burden…* (NO)

</details>

---

### **3b. Amplified**

YES when strengthened with intensifiers such as *highly, strongly, self-*, or *clearly.*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *We propose to evaluate Bounce Back Now (BBN), a novel, scalable, and highly **sustainable** technology-based intervention.*

</details>

---

### **3c. Broader context**

Often YES when co-occurring with additional positive utility terms such as *effective, scalable, feasible, practical, cost-effective, collaborative, robust,* or *innovative.*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *We seek HIV prevention strategies that are effective, safe, feasible, and **sustainable**, even in resource-limited settings.*

</details>

---

### **3d. Grounds**

YES when reasons are given for sustainability.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *We established a nearly self-**sustainable**, tuition-funded Epidemiology Masters in Peru…*

- *The proposed technology-based training tool should be highly **sustainable**, because it relies on community setting paraprofessional personnel…*

</details>

---

### **3e. Relative / Hedged**

YES when qualified with *may, could, potential, if successful, more, likely,* or comparative framing.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Population-level approaches are more **sustainable** and less costly compared to individual-level ones.*

</details>
