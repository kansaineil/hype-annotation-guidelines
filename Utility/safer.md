## **Safer — annotation guidance**

Adjective: **safer**  
Category: **UTILITY**

---

### **1. Value-judgement**

Usually YES. *Safer* evaluates an intervention, treatment, or approach as preferable.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *These studies will help to develop **safer** and more efficient methods for gene delivery which can be coupled to cardiac surgery.*

</details>

---

### **2. Hyperbolic**

No.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**

Usually NO. Mark YES only when *safer* adds an optional favourable attribute that can be removed while preserving the main research claim. Often this is not the case because safety is central.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *It is unclear which of these two catheters is **safer** for babies.* (NO)

</details>

---

### **3b. Amplified**

Rare.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3c. Broader context**

Commonly stacked with other utility adjectives.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *These advancements will allow clinicians and researchers to image pediatric subjects in a **safer**, more reliable and efficient manner.*

</details>

---

### **3d. Grounds**

Possible — safety may be justified with supporting data, comparisons, mechanisms, or outcomes.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3e. Relative / Hedged**

YES — inherently relative.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *These studies will help to develop **safer** and more efficient methods for gene delivery...*

</details>
