## **Ideal — annotation guidance**

Adjective: **ideal**  
Category: **UTILITY**

---

### **1. Value-judgement**

Usually YES — presents something as especially suitable, optimal, or best suited for a purpose.

NO in fixed or technical uses such as *ideal body weight, ideal observer model,* or *ideal-type.*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

**YES**

- *The SIV system is an **ideal** model to examine a variety of aspects of HIV pathogenesis.*

**NO**

- *Twenty normal male volunteers within 10 percent of their **ideal** body weight.*

</details>

---

### **2. Hyperbolic**

No.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**

Often YES in attributive or predicative promotional uses when removable without changing the core claim.

NO when *ideal* defines a technical standard, target condition, or comparison.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

**YES**

- *The Gordon Research Conference will be the **ideal** setting for information exchange and developing collaborations.*

**NO**

- *The **ideal** treatment would maximize stone fragmentation while minimizing tissue damage.*

</details>

---

### **3b. Amplified**

Rare — already superlative.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3c. Broader context**

Often YES — commonly appears with promotional terms such as *unique, excellent, compelling, innovative, state-of-the-art,* and *successful.*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Recent epidemiological studies offer compelling evidence that Ecuador is an **ideal** site to study onchocerciasis.*

</details>

---

### **3d. Grounds**

Often YES — suitability is frequently justified by a following *because/since/due to* clause or by listed features.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The model for this analysis is **ideal** because the facial nucleus is a homogeneous population of motor neurons...*

</details>

---

### **3e. Relative / Hedged**

Common — especially with *less than ideal, nearly ideal, more ideal, not ideal,* and *under ideal conditions.*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Alum, the only adjuvant approved for human use, is less than **ideal**.*

- *Under **ideal** conditions, determination of the sequence of terminal nucleotides is straightforward.*

</details>
