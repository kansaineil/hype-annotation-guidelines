## **Efficacious — annotation guidance**

Adjective: **efficacious**  
Category: **UTILITY**

---

### **1. Value-judgement**

Usually YES — positively evaluates a treatment, vaccine, drug, intervention, protocol, or strategy as producing the intended effect.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The hypothesis is that HIVIG will be **efficacious**, safe, and tolerable when administered with ZDV to HIV-infected pregnant women.*

</details>

---

### **2. Hyperbolic**

No.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**

Mixed. Use the deletion test strictly.

Mark YES when *efficacious* can be removed without changing the core claim, especially in broad development phrases.

Mark NO when *efficacious* is central to the claim, especially in predicate uses, comparative/superlative forms, negation/gap statements, trial questions, or safety/efficacy assessments.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

**YES**

- *develop practice guidelines for cost-effective and **efficacious** preventive treatment.* → *develop practice guidelines for preventive treatment.*

- *develop **efficacious** treatment paradigms.* → *develop treatment paradigms.*

**NO**

- *To determine whether estradiol is an **efficacious** treatment for the target disorders.*

- *there is no **efficacious** drug presently available to treat or prevent atherosclerosis or restenosis.*

</details>

---

### **3b. Amplified**

Common, especially *highly efficacious*.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The vaccine continues to be highly **efficacious** five years after administration to infants.*

</details>

---

### **3c. Broader context**

Often YES — frequently stacked with other utility/safety terms, especially *safe, tolerable, cost-effective, less toxic, selective,* and *efficient.*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Derivatized poly-lysines may have commercial potential as novel safe, relatively cheap and easy to manufacture, and **efficacious** agents for long-term treatment of immune disorders.*

</details>

---

### **3d. Grounds**

Often YES — frequently grounded through trial design, prior evidence, comparison, or stated conditions of use.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Pre-clinical studies have shown that murine A717 is therapeutically **efficacious** in preventing diabetic nephropathy.*

</details>

---

### **3e. Relative / Hedged**

Common — frequent in hypotheses, conditionals, comparisons, negation, uncertainty, and trial questions.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *If gene transfer in the nasal epithelium is sufficiently safe and **efficacious**, studies will extend to acute local administration into the lung of CF patients.*

</details>
