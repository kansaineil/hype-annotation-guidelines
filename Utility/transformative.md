## **Transformative — annotation guidance**

Adjective: **transformative**  
Category: **UTILITY**

---

### **1. Value-judgement**

YES — *transformative* strongly positively frames something as producing major impact. Common targets include *research, technologies, tools, discoveries, interventions, approaches, infrastructure, impact, opportunities,* and *advances.*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *This **transformative** technology will allow rapid extraction of systems information.* (YES)

- *The successful completion of the proposed work will prove **transformative** for deciphering disease mechanisms and result in the development of more effective disease treatments.* (YES)

- *The availability of two innovative new short-course TPT regimens offers a **transformative** opportunity to global TB control.* (YES)

</details>

---

### **2. Hyperbolic**

Usually YES.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *This product will have a **transformative** impact on Hunter Syndrome patients and, by extension, holds promise for application of this approach to other diseases.*

- *Achievement of the Center’s goal will be a **transformative** moment in modern medicine.*

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**

Usually YES — often removable without loss of propositional meaning:

- *transformative research → research*  
- *transformative impact → impact*  
- *transformative technology → technology*  
- *transformative opportunities → opportunities*  

NO when large-scale change itself is the principal claim or comparison.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *This proposal seeks to develop **transformative** native IM-MS technologies with high-resolution capabilities.* (YES)

- *The candidate’s Research Plan will make a significant and **transformative** contribution to the HIV research field.* (YES)

- *The mission will be accomplished through a series of coordinated and synergistic **transformative** changes in our educational and mentoring programs.* (NO)

- *The **transformative** nature of this proposal is that it allows us to translate molecular mechanisms identified at the cellular level.* (NO)

</details>

Possible.

<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

### **3c. Broader context**

Common.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The vision of the HICTR is to improve the health of Kansans by creating a novel and **transformative** translational research enterprise from bench to bedside to community.*

</details>

---

### **3d. Grounds**

Sometimes present.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The approach suggested here is **transformative** in that it completely eliminates the need for refrigeration and avoids unwanted freeze-thaw cycles.* (YES)

- *This proposal is **transformative** because the molecules and mechanisms for trafficking and distribution of heme are poorly understood.* (YES)

</details>

---

### **3e. Relative / Hedged**

Common — often appears in hedged, future-oriented forms such as *potentially transformative, could be transformative, would be transformative,* or *promises to develop a transformative...*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *This work proposed herein is fundamentally innovative and potentially **transformative**.*

- *It would be **transformative** if we could identify additional small molecules that could be repurposed to treat the outbreak.*

</details>
