## **Ready — annotation guidance**

Adjective: **ready**  
Category: **UTILITY**

---

### **1. Value-judgement**

YES when *ready* implies convenience, ease, immediate availability, or usability (e.g. *ready access, ready supply, ready source, ready market*). It presents something as accessible or useful.  

NO when indicating temporal state or preparedness.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Investigators will have **ready** access to clinical populations for study.* (YES)

- *This core provides a **ready** supply of animals.* (YES)

- *This core will provide all these services and will enable the researchers to have a **ready** supply of animals.* (YES)

- *The patient is **ready** for discharge home.* (NO)

</details>

---

### **2. Hyperbolic**

No.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**

Frequently YES for *ready + access / supply / source / market / reference* because *ready* often just adds *easy* or *immediate*, while the noun already carries the core meaning.

NO when *ready* expresses actual preparedness or stage progression.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Investigators also have **ready** access to patients…* → *Investigators also have access to patients…* (YES)

- *This core will provide… a **ready** supply of animals.* → *This core will provide… a supply of animals.* (YES)

- *The meeting was taped… to provide a **ready** reference for attendees.* → *…to provide a reference for attendees.* (YES)

- *We are now **ready** to test our hypotheses in vivo.* (NO)

</details>
