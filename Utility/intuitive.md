## **Intuitive — annotation guidance**

Adjective: **intuitive**  
Category: **UTILITY**

---

### **1. Value-judgement**

Mixed. YES when *intuitive* positively evaluates usability.  
NO when it refers to ordinary intuition/reasoning, especially in *counter-intuitive* uses or phrases such as *it seems intuitive that...*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The software (Simmune) offers an **intuitive** graphical interface combined with state-of-the-art simulation technology.* (YES)

- *We will also test the counter-intuitive hypothesis that mind-wandering will occur more often when people read in their dominant…* (NO)

</details>

---

### **2. Hyperbolic**

No.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**

Often YES in attributive descriptions when deletion leaves the core claim intact.  
Usually NO when *intuitive* is the main design goal, evaluation criterion, contrast, or predicate claim.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *eSNAP is an **intuitive**, user-friendly social network visualization tool designed for clinical use.* (YES)

- *Intuitive operation remains a challenge that only increases with task complexity…* (NO)

</details>

---

### **3b. Amplified**

Often YES — common boosters include *very* and *highly.*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The users of cBio analytical methods find them very **intuitive** and thereby useful.*

</details>

---

### **3c. Broader context**

Frequently co-occurs with usability adjectives.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *eSNAP is an **intuitive**, user-friendly social network visualization tool designed for clinical use.*

</details>

---

### **3d. Grounds**

Occasionally YES — when usability or naturalness is explicitly justified.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Treatments delivered via mobile device are able to overcome this barrier through the use of **intuitive** graphical interfaces that eliminate the need for text or language-based instruction.*

</details>

---

### **3e. Relative / Hedged**

Often YES — includes *more intuitive, counter-intuitive, seems intuitive, may be intuitive,* and negated forms.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *While this approach would seem **intuitive**, current conceptualizations of PrEP implementation narrowly use a clinical model focused on individualized intervention…*

</details>
