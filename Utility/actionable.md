## **Actionable — annotation guidance**

Adjective: **actionable**  
Category: **UTILITY**

---

### **1. Value-judgement**  
Usually YES — positively frames something as useful for decision-making or intervention.

Typical targets: *targets, solutions, next steps, insights, information, data, evidence, recommendations, findings, biomarkers, mutations, interventions.*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *This effort explores the hypothesis that a low-cost, non-intrusive home monitoring system can provide **actionable** information on the welfare of a home's occupant, i.e., an "Electronic Concerned Neighbor".*

</details>

---

### **2. Hyperbolic**

No.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**

Most often YES — removable without changing core meaning.

NO when it adds a meaningful constraint.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

**YES**

- ***actionable** insights → insights*

- ***actionable** recommendations → recommendations*

- ***actionable** information → information*

- *The use of multiple data sources and multiple measures of infections, and the use of patient-level data which brings risk-adjustment under the investigators control, will also allow for the triangulation of results and identification of **actionable** recommendations with promise for reducing HAIs.*

**NO**

- *clinically **actionable** mutations*

- *no **actionable** targets were found*

</details>

---

### **3b. Amplified**

Rare.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Most importantly, NTRK fusions are highly **actionable** in the clinic.*

</details>

### **3d. Grounds**

Possible but not common.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Although NIH has made more general recommendations for revising the Listings to SSA in past reports, we believe that the current set of recommendations is more **actionable** due to the level of specificity and associated examples that are provided.*

</details>

---

### **3e. Relative / Hedged**

Possible but not common.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Although GWAS on common variants have made strides in identifying >1,000 signals for obesity-related traits, these studies are inherently limited without further translation into more **actionable** findings.*

</details>
