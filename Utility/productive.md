## **Productive — annotation guidance**

Adjective: **productive**  
Category: **UTILITY**

---

### **1. Value-judgement**

YES when *productive* positively evaluates usefulness, effectiveness, or beneficial output.  
NO when used literally or technically (e.g. virology: *productive infection*, *productive life cycle*).

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The goal of this seminar series is to generate new ideas, foster **productive** interactions and encourage collaborations between basic research investigators and clinicians across the Campus community.* (YES)

- *Productive infection was demonstrated by reverse transcriptase-polymerase chain reaction analysis of host cell mRNA.* (NO)

</details>

---

### **2. Hyperbolic**

No.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**

Often YES for institutional or career praise where deletion leaves the core claim intact.  
NO when *productive* contributes necessary content, especially in technical viral or biological uses.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The investigators have a strong history of prior highly **productive** collaborative research with each other and are expert in the investigative areas of allergy and immunology, cell and molecular biology, physiology, morphology, psychology, medical geography, and biostatistics necessary to address these basic questions on the pathobiology of asthma.*

</details>

---

### **3b. Amplified**

Often YES — strengthened by *highly, very, most, particularly, intensely,* or *strongly.*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The investigators involved in the SCOR are innovative, dynamic, and very **productive**.*

</details>

---

### **3c. Broader context**

YES when surrounded by other promotional language or adjective stacking.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Investigators supported by this Core have a longstanding history of **productive** collaborations in basic and clinical research on age-associated neurodegenerative disorders.*

</details>

---

### **3d. Grounds**

YES when productivity is justified with evidence such as publications, outputs, achievements, or measurable outcomes.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *particularly **productive** over the past four years, accounting for 60 research reports, 46 reviews…*

</details>

---

### **3e. Relative / Hedged**

YES when qualified, comparative, conditional, or framed as possibility rather than fact.

Examples include: *should prove productive, more productive,* and *potentially productive.*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *This single phase of the study should be sufficiently **productive** to warrant its funding.*

</details>
