## **Deployable — annotation guidance**

Adjective: **deployable**  
Category: **UTILITY**

---

### **1. Value-judgement**  

YES when *deployable* positively frames usefulness or readiness.

NO when used in a purely technical/descriptive sense (rare).

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

**YES**

- ***deployable** solution*

- ***deployable** system*

- *However, the potential for using expression signatures for cancer prognosis and treatment selection is hampered by lack of readily **deployable** test kits with the accuracy, low RNA requirement and inter-site concordance required for routine clinical use.*

</details>

---

### **2. Hyperbolic**

No.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**

YES when removable without changing core meaning.

NO when it adds a meaningful constraint.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

**YES**

- ***deployable** system → system*

- ***deployable** model → model*

**NO**

- ***deployable** in low-resource settings*

- ***deployable** on existing infrastructure*

- *clinically **deployable** (vs. lab-only)*

- *f NIR imaging has been demonstrated as a sensitive method to map functional activities in the healthy human motor cortex and is relatively immune from patient motion artifacts, is cost effective, and can be widely **deployable** in a clinical setting.* (NO)

- *This modified ISF collection device (i.e., WICK) will be simple-to-operate and **deployable** in the lab and in the field, thereby enabling many researchers to collect and study ISF biomarkers.* (NO)

</details>

---

### **3b. Amplified**

Often YES — intensified by adverbs.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *easily **deployable***

- *rapidly **deployable***

- *widely **deployable***

- *fully **deployable***

- *We then will assess whether this new information can translate into readily **deployable**, topical therapies for patients after their initial optimization in ARCI HEEs, and in disease-appropriate animal models.*

</details>
