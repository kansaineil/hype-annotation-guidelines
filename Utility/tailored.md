## **Tailored — annotation guidance**

Adjective: **tailored**  
Category: **UTILITY**

---

### **1. Value-judgement**

Usually YES — positively frames something as adapted to a specific purpose. Common targets include *interventions, training plans, messages, materials, treatment plans, education, curricula, strategies,* and *programs.*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Individualized training plans will be **tailored** to the academic background of each student but will include four required courses in oncology.*

</details>

---

### **2. Hyperbolic**

No.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

NA

</details>

---

### **3a. Gratuitous**

Often YES when *tailored* simply adds positive framing and can be removed without changing the core claim: *tailored messages → messages*; *tailored intervention → intervention.*

NO when the adaptation itself is the main criterion or comparison.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *We will modify our existing PEMT to develop an interactive, **tailored**, touch screen, bilingual breast-feeding educational program specifically designed to deliver breast-feeding education.* (YES)

- *Individualized training plans will be **tailored** to the academic background of each student but will include four required courses in oncology.* (NO)

- *We expect that the **tailored** post-session DCS administration condition will outperform the pre-session DCS administration, placebo administration, and non-tailored post-session DCS administration.* (NO)

- *It will also be **tailored** to patients’ unique sleep patterns, adjusting recommendations based on automatically gathered objective sleep data.* (NO)

- *These services provided can be **tailored** to suit experimental designs in a variety of fields.* (NO)

</details>

---

### **3b. Amplified**

Possible — common modifiers include *highly, individually,* and *specifically.*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Our system can generate highly **tailored** follow-up questions and recommendations for the physician as well as personalized educational materials.*

</details>

---

### **3c. Broader context**

Common. Often appears with broader promotional utility language such as *personalized, adaptive, culturally relevant, innovative, effective, interactive, low burden,* and *accessible.*

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Automated, **tailored**, low burden, and easily accessible interventions may be used to help socioeconomically disadvantaged smokers.*

</details>

---

### **3d. Grounds**

Possible when the sentence explains the basis for tailoring, such as needs, preferences, risk, background, culture, sleep patterns, or patient attributes.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *It will also be **tailored** to patients’ unique sleep patterns, adjusting recommendations based on automatically gathered objective sleep data.*

</details>

---

### **3e. Relative / Hedged**

Possible. Often appears in aims, planned development, or conditional claims where usefulness is not presented as established.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *If successful, our Smartphone approach will provide a novel, efficacious and scalable intervention for weight management in pregnant women.*

</details>
