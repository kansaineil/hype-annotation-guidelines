## Accurate — annotation guidance summary

| Dimension | Typical behaviour | Hype risk | Diagnostic cue | Example (Not hype) | Example (Hype) |
|----------|------------------|-----------|----------------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Value-judgement** | Usually YES | Medium | Implies correctness / success | _(to add)_ | _(to add)_ |
| **Hyperbolic** | Rarely hyperbolic | Low | Not an extremity adjective | — | — |
| **Gratuitous** | Depends on noun semantics | Variable | Does removing *accurate* change meaning? | *accurate measurement* → changes meaning | *accurate diagnosis* → meaning unchanged |
| **Noun type** | See three-way split below | — | Guides Step 3a decision | — | — |
| **Amplified** | Common | High | Modified by boosters or stance verbs | _(to add: e.g., comparative with evidence)_ | *highly accurate*, *demonstrated accurate* |
| **Broader context** | Often stacked | Medium–High | Co-occurs with promotional adjectives | _(to add)_ | *robust and accurate methods* |
| **Grounds (justified?)** | Often present | Low if YES | Evidence supports the claim | *accuracy validated using datasets* | _(to add: lack of grounds)_ |
| **Relative / hedged** | Common | Low if YES | Comparative / uncertain framing | *more accurate than existing tools* | _(to add: absolute assertion)_ |
