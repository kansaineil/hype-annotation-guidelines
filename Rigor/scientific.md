### **1. Value-judgement**  
Mixed.

- **Value-judgement** when used as a prestige marker and removable without changing core propositional meaning.  
  Typical epistemic / cognitive nouns: *understanding, knowledge, information, insight(s), background(s), rationale, premise, foundation, basis, idea(s), perspective, view(s), approach*.  
- **Not a value-judgement** when defining a domain, role, institutional category, or standard, where removal changes reference or scope.  
  Typical organizational / standards / institutional nouns: *community, workforce, career, leadership, training, program, infrastructure, committee, advisory board, director, staff, collaboration, conference, journal, publication, communication, integrity, rigor, ethics, method, computing, management, oversight*.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Cedars-Sinai is unique in that it is the only community hospital awarded SCOR-IHD grant in the fifteen year history of the NIH program, reflecting its unique capability to bridge the critical gap between **scientific** innovations and their effective implementation in clinical medicine.* (value-judgement)

- *Given its diverse and multifactorial nature, it is critical to understand the etiology and progression of this insidious brain disorder at multiple dimensions, including clinically and neuropathologically, and to employ novel **scientific** approaches so that we can advance knowledge and better diagnose, prevent and cure the disease.* (NOT value-judgement)

</details>

### **3a. Gratuitous**  
Sometimes gratuitous when modifying abstract epistemic nouns where *scientific* is already presupposed or contextually clear (e.g., *understanding, knowledge, insight, merit, importance, value, progress, advance, achievement*).

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The research findings will further **scientific** insights into the mechanisms of cancer prevention and develop novel potential biomarkers as determinants of response.* (gratuitous)

</details>
